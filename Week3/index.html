Week 3: Hands tools and Fabrication


This week, I'm not entirely sure I fulfilled the prompt, unfortunately, but I did make something that moves! I was focused on a moving kinect piece rather than one of joints. But I did use a ton of tools and worked with many different materials, such as brass, acyrillic, wood. And I used many of the tools in the lab to cut these materials. Which I think was the heart of this week...using the hand tools and making something new.
Without further ado, I made a Alexander Calder Mobile inspired kinetic sculpture.

My original sketch included another layer that included working sound objects, such as a contact mic and such. This way they could be plugged in as they rotated to create a sound scape, but sadly time was not on my side. Hopefully I can continue this later this semester.

But as it is, I'm pretty happy with the end result in terms of design. The arduino and motors are set onto a piece of wood that I cut out. The materials of the mobile are brass tubes that I used the drill to cut holes into so that I could thread smaller pieces of brass through. At the ends of the small brass legs are piezo disks that matched in color to the rest of the mobile as well had the sound element of the original sketch.

To get the motor to work, I used a L298N Motor driver and a small DC Motor. This is hooked up to an arduino UNO which then talks to Max MSP to tell it to run the motor. Max MSP controls the speed and direction of the motor.

basic steps to get Max MSP to talk to Arduino UNO

  1. open Arduino IDE and download Firmate Standard onto the board. More about the library here
  2. Open Max MSP, a premade template exists called Maxuino, which is what I'm using
  3. Go through the steps to initialize the pins in use on the board
That's the general idea and the way I am operating this mobile.
